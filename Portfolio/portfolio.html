Systems Modelling Research

I worked as part of a four person team to model the effects of disorder and anisotropy on cubic lattices. My primary role was to create the model needed to run the tests. This model outputted a Hamiltonian matrix, shown in the first figure to the right.

The Hamiltonian could then be used to construct a set of molecular orbital energy levels, shown in the second figure. From the spacing patterns of the orbitals, conclusions could be made about the effect of the modelled disorder and anisotropy. For example, the irregularity of the spacing in the figure shown demonstrates that anisotropy reduces the conductance of the lattice. Other tests were also run, and the full presentation of these findings can be seen here.

One of the most interesting, unanticipated results was that the pattern exhibited by the Hamiltonian made it possible to create a robust model for a theoretical fourth dimensional lattice. This lattice had fairly similar behavior, albeit with a greater number of energy levels and an exponentially larger Hamiltonian matrix.
